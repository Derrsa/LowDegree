How to bring the temperature down?

First of all, we need to find out what kind of fever we have.
There are two types of fever: White fever and Red fever

White fever:
  • The skin is pale, marbled, with a bluish tinge of nail beds and lips
  • The limbs are cold to the touch
  • The symptom of a "white spot" is positive when pressing on the skin
  • Behavior is disturbed (the baby becomes indifferent, sluggish), delirium and convulsions are possible
What we gonna do with white fever?
  • Undress the baby as much as possible;
  • Apply a cool wet bandage on the forehead;
  • Regularly ventilate the room (without the formation of drafts); blow with a fan;
  • Wipe the body with room temperature water;
  • Apply cold to the projection of large vessels (in the axillary and inguinal areas, on the side of the neck) and the liver area; drink plenty of water.
  • And if fever more than 38.5 degree, we need to take antipyretic
Red fever:
  • Reddish skin – hot and moist to the touch
  • Warm limbs
  • Rapid pulse and breathing against the background of an increase in temperature
  • The child's behavior does not change.
What we gonna do with red fever?
  • Undress the baby as much as possible;
  • Apply a cool wet bandage on the forehead;
  • Regularly ventilate the room (without the formation of drafts); blow with a fan;
  • Wipe the body with room temperature water;
  • Apply cold to the projection of large vessels (in the axillary and inguinal areas, on the side of the neck) and the liver area; drink plenty of water.
  • And if fever more than 38.5 degree, we need to take antipyretic
